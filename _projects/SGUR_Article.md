## Article Presentation

This article explores the results of a UNICEF participatory survey on children's rights, with a specific focus on West Africa. It analyzes how these surveys serve as vital tools for raising awareness, giving young people a voice, and highlighting critical social issues such as sexual violence and human trafficking. Through various examples and data, the piece contrasts the potential of participatory tools to drive justice against their limitations regarding representativeness and political implementation.
